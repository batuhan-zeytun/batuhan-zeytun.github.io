Top Chess Influencers

Magnus Carlsen

Magnus Carlsen, born in 1990 in Tønsberg, Norway, became a grandmaster at just 13 years old. He rose to international stardom when he became World Chess Champion in 2013, and went on to defend his title multiple times until 2023.
In 2014, he achieved a peak rating of 2882 — the highest in chess history. Carlsen is celebrated not only for his dominance over the board but also for modernizing the game through mobile apps, fantasy leagues, and online tournaments.

Fabiano Caruana

Fabiano Caruana, born in 1992 in Miami, Florida, became a grandmaster at age 14. He is best known for his incredible performance in the 2018 World Championship match against Magnus Carlsen, where all classical games ended in draws — a first in championship history.
Caruana has been a consistent top-10 player globally, praised for his deep opening preparation, precise calculation, and calm demeanor under pressure. He continues to represent the U.S. in elite-level tournaments and Olympiads.

Hikaru Nakamura

Hikaru Nakamura, born in 1987 in Japan and raised in the U.S., is a five-time U.S. Champion and one of the world’s best blitz and bullet players. He earned his grandmaster title at 15 and has been a top figure in fast chess formats ever since.
Beyond traditional tournaments, Nakamura has become the face of online chess. With millions of followers across Twitch and YouTube, he has helped introduce chess to younger audiences and played a major role in the game’s streaming renaissance.
